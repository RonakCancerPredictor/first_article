Dr. Sargol Aminnezhad

PhD in Biotechnology & Microbiology | Researcher at Alzahra University, Iran

Biography

Dr. Sargol Aminnezhad is a distinguished Iranian scientist known for her work in biotechnology, microbiology, and immunoinformatics. She currently serves as a faculty member in the Department of Microbiology at Alzahra University in Tehran. Her research focuses on harnessing the power of beneficial microbes, designing novel vaccine candidates using bioinformatics tools, and exploring plant-based antimicrobial agents. Dr. Aminnezhad’s work has contributed to the development of innovative treatments and prevention strategies for antibiotic-resistant bacterial infections. Her publications demonstrate a strong interdisciplinary approach, integrating laboratory microbiology with advanced computational biology techniques.

Bioinformatics Contributions

In recent years, Dr. Aminnezhad has incorporated computational biology approaches into her research, applying immunoinformatics tools for epitope prediction, molecular docking, and in silico vaccine design. Her bioinformatics-driven studies offer promising directions in the rational design of therapeutics and diagnostics, particularly against multidrug-resistant pathogens. This approach reduces reliance on trial-and-error experimentation and accelerates the discovery process.

She has also co-authored studies involving the modeling of 3D protein structures, prediction of immune system responses, and the integration of omics data to uncover novel therapeutic targets. Her work stands at the intersection of life sciences and computational technologies, helping to bridge the gap between lab research and clinical application.
