Dr. Sargol Aminnezhad

PhD in Biotechnology & Microbiology | Researcher at Alzahra University, Iran

Biography

Dr. Sargol Aminnezhad is a dedicated researcher specializing in biotechnology and microbiology. She is currently affiliated with the Department of Microbiology at Alzahra University in Tehran, Iran. Her research interests encompass molecular biology, immunoinformatics, antimicrobial agents, and vaccine design. Dr. Aminnezhad has contributed significantly to the scientific community through her publications and collaborative research efforts.

Bioinformatics Contributions

Synergistic Effects of Supernatant from Lactobacillus plantarum Cultured Medium and Conventional Antibiotics Against Pseudomonas aeruginosa

Published: December 2012

Authors: Sargol Aminnezhad, N. Rahimifard, R.K. Kermanshahi, F. Bagheri

Summary: This study investigates the synergistic effects of Lactobacillus plantarum supernatant combined with conventional antibiotics against Pseudomonas aeruginosa, highlighting potential alternative therapeutic strategies.

Evaluation of Synergistic Interactions Between Cell-Free Supernatant of Lactobacillus Strains and Amikacin and Gentamicin Against Pseudomonas aeruginosa

Published: April 2015

Authors: Sargol Aminnezhad, Rouha Kasra Kermanshahi, Reza Ranjbar

Summary: This research evaluates the synergistic interactions between cell-free supernatants of Lactobacillus strains and antibiotics amikacin and gentamicin, offering insights into combating antibiotic resistance.

Effect of Althaea officinalis Extract on Growth and Biofilm Formation in Pseudomonas aeruginosa

Published: September 2016

Authors: Sargol Aminnezhad, R.K. Kermanshahi, Reza Ranjbar

Summary: This study examines the impact of Althaea officinalis extract on the growth and biofilm formation of Pseudomonas aeruginosa, suggesting its potential as a therapeutic agent.

Immunoinformatics Design of Multivalent Chimeric Vaccine for Modulation of the Immune System in Pseudomonas aeruginosa Infection

Published: November 2020

Authors: Sargol Aminnezhad, Ahya Abdi-Ali, Tooba Ghazanfari, Mojgan Bandehpour, Mahboobe Zarrabi

Summary: This paper presents an immunoinformatics approach to designing a multivalent chimeric vaccine aimed at modulating the immune response in Pseudomonas aeruginosa infections.

Applications of Biocompatible Polymeric Nanomaterials in Three-Dimensional Cell Culture Models

Published: 2025

Authors: Sargol Aminnezhad, Nabaz Hamarashid Hama, et al.

Summary: This review discusses the applications of biocompatible polymeric nanomaterials in three-dimensional cell culture models, emphasizing their role in tissue engineering and regenerative medicine.
